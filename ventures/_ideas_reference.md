# Weekend Business Ideas — Reference Document

**Created:** 2026-03-20
**Context:** Ryan asked for 3 simplest businesses launchable in 48 hours. Subagent debate generated and stress-tested multiple ideas. These 4 survived as the most viable.

## Idea 1: Google Business Profile Optimization

**What it is:** Audit and fix local businesses' Google Maps listings — hours, photos, description, categories, posts, review requests.

**Price:** $150-300 per optimization

**Weekend launch:**
- Saturday: Research GBP best practices (30 min). Find 20 businesses with incomplete profiles. Send outreach.
- Sunday: Follow up, close 1-2 clients, do the optimizations.

**First 10 customers:** Search "[service] near me" on Google Maps. Find businesses with no photos, sparse info, low reviews. Call/email: "Your Google listing is incomplete. I'll optimize it for $200."

**Why it works:** Purely mechanical — follow Google's checklist. Business owners don't have time. $200 is easy yes.

**Risks:** Requires outbound sales. Some clients may expect ongoing management.

**Verdict from debate:** Solid service business. No expertise required. Scalable once systemized.

## Idea 2: Website Speed Audit + Fix List

**What it is:** Run Google PageSpeed Insights on small business websites. Deliver 1-page PDF with score + top 5 fixes. Upsell implementation.

**Price:** $50-100 per audit, $200+ for implementation

**Weekend launch:**
- Saturday: Audit 30 local business sites (30 min total). Email all 30 with free audit as hook.
- Sunday: Follow up. Close 3-5 audits + 1-2 implementations.

**First 10 customers:** Find local business websites (contractors, dentists, salons). Run PageSpeed Insights. Screenshot results. Email: "Your site is slow — hurting Google rankings. Here's what's wrong."

**Why it works:** Google is the expert, not you. You're just the messenger. Tool is free. Fast to deliver.

**Risks:** Low price point requires volume. Implementation upsell requires technical knowledge or a partner.

**Verdict from debate:** Good hook, but audit-only is a commodity. Real money is in implementation.

## Idea 3: Competitor Ad Spy Reports

**What it is:** Use Facebook Ad Library (free) to find what ads competitors are running. Deliver report: "Top 10 Ads in [Industry] Right Now."

**Price:** $97-147 per report

**Weekend launch:**
- Saturday: Pick 2-3 niches. Create sample reports as portfolio.
- Sunday: Post in Facebook groups for those niches. DM commenters.

**First 10 customers:** Post in niche Facebook groups: "I analyzed top 20 ads in [industry]. Report is $97." DM anyone who engages.

**Why it works:** Information arbitrage — business owners don't know Ad Library exists. You're curating public info into actionable format.

**Risks:** Niche selection matters. Some industries have few active ads. One-time purchase, not recurring.

**Verdict from debate:** Solid info product. Low cost. Marketing is the bottleneck (same as all digital products).

## Idea 4: Niche SOP Library (Direct Sales Version)

**What it is:** Industry-specific SOP bundles (hiring, onboarding, customer service, refunds, daily open/close) for one vertical — e.g., cleaning businesses, salons, landscaping.

**Price:** $47 per bundle (5-8 SOPs), $67 for full library (15+ SOPs)

**Weekend launch:**
- Saturday: Pick ONE industry. Write 5-8 core SOPs in Google Docs. Export as PDFs.
- Sunday: Find 50 business owners in Facebook groups for that industry. DM with free sample offer.

**First 10 customers:** 50 DMs at 2-3% conversion = 1-2 sales. Post free sample in groups with link to buy full pack.

**Why it works:** Generic SOPs compete with free. Industry-specific SOPs save 5+ hours of work. Direct outreach fixes Gumroad discovery problem.

**Risks:** Requires writing + research time. Manual outreach is labor-intensive. Competes with free templates.

**Verdict from debate:** Critic's recommended fix for the failed Gumroad version. Direct sales is the only way this works.
