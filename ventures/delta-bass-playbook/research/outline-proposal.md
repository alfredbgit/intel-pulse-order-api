# The Delta Playbook — Proposed Outline

**Working title:** "The Delta Playbook: All you need to know about catching Bass on the California Delta"

**Format:** PDF guide, ~40-60 pages, tactical and reference-friendly

---

## Proposed Structure

### Introduction (2-3 pages)
- Welcome to the Delta — why this fishery is special
- How to use this playbook (reference guide, not a novel)
- What's covered / what's not

---

### Chapter 1: The Delta Explained (5-7 pages)
- Geography overview — what is the California Delta?
- Key regions: Stockton, Sacramento, Central Delta, South Delta
- Tides 101: why they matter, how to read them, running the tide vs. holding
- Water types: river channels, sloughs, docks, vegetation
- The two species: Largemouth vs. Smallmouth (where, when, how they differ)

---

### Chapter 2: Seasonal Patterns (10-12 pages)
*The single most important chapter — when to do what*

- **Winter (Dec-Feb):** Where they are, what they're doing, how to catch them
- **Prespawn (Feb-Mar):** The bite window, transition areas, key signs
- **Spawn (Mar-May):** Bed fishing, spotting, approach, release
- **Post-Spawn (May-Jun):** Recovery patterns, shallow to deep transition
- **Summer (Jul-Sep):** Deep structure, early/late bites, heat strategies
- **Fall (Oct-Nov):** Feeding frenzies, baitfish patterns, topwater window

---

### Chapter 3: Locations & Structure (8-10 pages)
*Where to actually go*

- Docks: types, depths, seasons, how to work them
- Vegetation: hydrilla, lily pads, emergent grass — punching through mats
- River channels & drop-offs: reading sonar, offshore structure
- Buoys & current breaks: why fish hold here
- Wind-blown points & banks: when the bite turns on
- Specific areas to know: Little Mandeville, Rio Vista, Bethel Island, Big Break, etc.

---

### Chapter 4: Techniques That Work (10-12 pages)
*Step-by-step how-to for each method*

- **Punching:** Setup, approach, the punch itself, setting the hook
- **Flipping & Pitching:** When and how
- **Spinnerbait fishing:** Retrieval speeds, colors, wind considerations
- **Crankbaits:** Shallow vs. deep, deflection, seasonal timing
- **Topwater:** Frogs, Whopper Ploppers, buzzbaits — when the surface bite fires
- **Swimbaits:** Big baits for big fish, retrieve, gear
- **Jigs:** Football, flipping, punching — variations
- **Soft plastics:** Yamamoto senkos, creature baits, wacky rig

---

### Chapter 5: Tackle & Gear (5-7 pages)
*What to bring*

- Rods: power, action, length recommendations by technique
- Reels: gear ratio basics
- Line: braid vs. fluorocarbon vs. monofilament — when to use what
- Terminal tackle: hooks, weights, swivels
- Lure recommendations by season (the "go-to" list)
- Electronics: sonar basics for Delta fishing
- Boat vs. bank: does it matter?

---

### Chapter 6: Reading Conditions (4-5 pages)
*Making decisions on the water*

- Tide charts: how to use them, what the numbers mean
- Weather fronts: barometric pressure, wind, cloud cover
- Water clarity: stained vs. clear, adjustments
- Time of day: dawn, dusk, midday — when to push vs. when to pause

---

### Chapter 7: Quick Reference Cards (3-4 pages)
*Tear-out style reference*

- Seasonal lure selector chart
- Tide decision tree (high/low/moving — where to go)
- "If you only have 2 hours..." scenarios
- Common mistakes and how to fix them

---

### Appendix (2-3 pages)
- Resources: tide charts, maps, local shops
- Video references: QR codes linking to key technique videos from the source library
- Notes section
